\begin{abstract}
Neuron-aware instruction-tuning data selection (NAIT) scores a candidate example by
collapsing its decoder-layer activations to a per-layer principal direction, summing
the layer projections into a single scalar, and selecting the top-$k$. That sum
discards \emph{which} layer carried the evidence and tunes every layer uniformly. We
ask whether the per-layer axis NAIT already computes can be turned into a
\emph{decision variable} that drives both \emph{which} examples to keep \emph{and}
\emph{where} each example's gradient should land---and whether doing so beats NAIT at a
compute-matched budget. \textbf{LATTICE-R} derives, from one per-example
endpoint+NAIT-residualized trajectory attribution $\psivec(x)\in\R^{|\Aset|}$, two
coupled readouts: the data-selection score is the \emph{sum} $\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}
\psi_\ell(x)$ and the LoRA layer-routing policy is the \emph{support}
$\mathrm{supp}(\psivec(x))$, tied by the exact identity $\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\psi_\ell(x)
= \hat{\bm\beta}_T\cdot\Ttil(x)$. We make the routing object a well-posed estimand---a
\emph{pool-conditional policy value} $\Vpol(\pi)=\E_{\mathrm{seed}}[\Utr(\pi)\mid\text{locked
pools}]$ over whole training runs, so there is no per-example potential outcome and no
cross-example SUTVA violation---and we pre-register an interventional routing test
\emph{that would certify} whether the coupling is load-bearing: six real masked-LoRA
training arms, decided by an \emph{intersection-union test} (each contrast at the marginal
level, no multiplicity penalty). The Intersection-Union construction
(Berger 1982) is exact \emph{conditional on} valid level-$\alpha$ component tests; the
implemented per-contrast inference is asymptotic (paired studentized bootstrap-$t$), so the
finite-sample level is approximate. (These runs are not yet executed.) The headline
claim is that LATTICE-R beats a \emph{faithfully reproduced} NAIT comparator (its
layer-sum scored over the full released decoder set) and every locked baseline on
retention-adjusted target utility at a \emph{measured} compute-matched budget. A
ridge-FWL/double-ML mechanism certificate, computed out-of-sample through a frozen
nuisance map, licenses the method non-circularly; a three-pool firewall, a measured
compute ledger, and a closed-testing graph with an in-document FWER bound complete the
plan. This is a \emph{Stage-1 Registered Report}: method, theory, and analysis plan are
locked \emph{before} data collection. We report \textbf{no} experimental numbers; every
results slot is an explicit placeholder. The v5 modules \emph{are implemented} as
additive do-not-run pure-Python with a green unit harness of \textbf{93 passing}
formula-evaluation tests (\texttt{server.authorized=false}, no run executed); those are
code-correctness checks, not evidence.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% =====================================================================

\paragraph{The gap.}
Instruction tuning improves how language models follow instructions, but its returns
depend heavily on \emph{which} examples are used~\cite{zhou2023lima,cao2023instruction}.
The base method we build on, Neuron-Aware Data Selection (NAIT)~\cite{nait2026}, reads
decoder-layer activations on in-domain data, reduces each layer to a sign-aligned
principal direction $v_\ell$, scores a candidate by the \emph{layer-sum projection}
$s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}(y)=\sum_{\ell=1}^{L}\big(A^{(\ell)}(y)\cdot v_\ell\big)$, and selects
the top-$k$ (base Algorithm~1, Eqs.~1--6). Two structural commitments are baked in.
\textbf{(i) Layer collapse to a sum, then top-$k$:} the layer-sum destroys \emph{which}
layer carried the alignment evidence, and the method tunes \emph{all} parameters
uniformly. \textbf{(ii) Selection only:} NAIT answers ``which examples,'' never ``given
this example's internal evidence, \emph{where} should its gradient land.'' The
per-layer axis is computed and then discarded. The open question is whether keeping
that axis as a decision variable---using one attribution to choose both the examples
\emph{and} the per-example tuning footprint---predicts downstream \emph{utility} at a
\emph{compute-matched} budget, against a faithfully reproduced NAIT and a strong field
of selectors that, recent work warns, often fail to beat random~\cite{diddee2024chasing}.

\paragraph{Why this is hard to do honestly.}
A naive ``trajectory/routing features beat endpoints'' comparison is easy to get wrong
in four ways. \textbf{(a) Re-encoding:} a trajectory or routing summary can silently
re-encode the endpoint signature, so a raw gain is an artifact of endpoint variance, not
new information. We control the \emph{full} endpoint geometry by ridge orthogonalization,
not a low-rank shadow. \textbf{(b) An ill-posed routing estimand:} a per-example
``turn layer $\ell$ off'' contrast under fixed capacity is \emph{not} a single potential
outcome---freeing rank $r_\ell$ forces a redistribution with no canonical rule---and a
shared LoRA run violates cross-example SUTVA. We therefore make the estimand a
\emph{policy value} over whole runs (the treated unit is the run, not the example).
\textbf{(c) Invalid out-of-sample tests:} a nested-model $F$ on out-of-sample residual
sums of squares has no valid reference; ``beats every control'' tested with a shared
min-over-controls (max-$T$) quantile is anti-conservative at the least-favorable
configuration. We use a cross-fit conditional-null permutation for the mechanism
certificate and an \emph{intersection-union test}~\cite{berger1982iut} for every
``beats all'' decision. \textbf{(d) Uncounted compute:} equal LoRA rank is not equal
compute. We require a \emph{measured} compute-matched ledger (params, optimizer slots,
realized FLOPs, skip-flag) and forbid ``savings'' without a measured reduction.

\paragraph{Our approach (LATTICE-R).}
We fit one per-example endpoint+NAIT-residualized trajectory attribution
$\psivec(x)\in\R^{|\Aset|}$ over an anchor-layer set $\Aset$ ($|\Aset|=8$) and read it
\emph{twice}: the data-selection score is the \emph{sum} $\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\psi_\ell(x)$
and the LoRA layer-routing \emph{policy} $\pi_\psi$ allocates a fixed rank budget $\Rtot$
\emph{over $\Aset$} to the \emph{support} of $\psivec(x)$, holding the non-anchor layers
$\Lset\setminus\Aset$ at a fixed baseline rank $r_0$ shared by every arm. The coupling
identity (Sec.~\ref{sec:method})
\[
\textstyle\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\psi_\ell(x)=\hat{\bm\beta}_T\cdot\Ttil(x)
\]
ties the two readouts to \emph{one} orthogonalized coefficient $\hat{\bm\beta}_T$ on
\emph{one} out-of-sample residual $\Ttil(x)=\Tmat(x)-\Zmat(x)\Blam$. We pre-register an
\emph{interventional routing test} (Gate R1) to certify the coupling once the authorized
run executes: six real masked-LoRA arms over $\Aset$ ($\pi_\psi$, uniform,
shuffled-$\psi$, random-mask, global-surgical, AdaLoRA), all compute-matched, decided by
an intersection-union test of the pool-conditional policy-value gaps. The headline (Gate
R2) is the pre-registered claim that LATTICE-R beats a
faithful NAIT comparator scored over the full $\Lset$ and every locked baseline on
retention-adjusted utility at a \emph{measured} matched budget.

\paragraph{Position relative to NAIT, in one line.}
\emph{NAIT sums the layer axis away (over all $\Lset$) and tunes uniformly; LATTICE-R
keeps the layer axis as a per-example decision variable for selection \emph{and}
capacity allocation over $\Aset$, derives both from one endpoint+NAIT-residualized
attribution, and is \emph{designed to test}, under real matched-compute training runs
that the authorized run will execute, whether the routing policy's value
$\Vpol(\pi_\psi)$ beats every prior-art arm (random, AdaLoRA, surgical, NeFT, MoE-routing)
at a compute-matched budget---each by its own marginal-$\alpha$ margin bound. (Runs are
not yet executed; this is the pre-registered test, not a demonstrated result.)}

\paragraph{Falsifiable central claim.}
\begin{quote}\itshape
One endpoint+NAIT-residualized trajectory attribution $\psivec$ drives both data
selection (the sum) and LoRA layer routing (the support); at a measured compute-matched
budget, the routing policy's pool-conditional value $\Vpol(\pi_\psi)$ exceeds that of
every capacity-matched control by margin $\delta_{R1}$ (each control beaten by its own
marginal-$\alpha$ lower bound), and the resulting selection+routing method beats a
faithfully reproduced NAIT (scored over the full $\Lset$) and every locked baseline on
retention-adjusted target utility.
\end{quote}
This is decisively falsifiable: if the residual attribution is null (Gate R0), or
routing fails to beat every control (Gate R1), or the matched-budget win fails
(Gate R2), the pre-registered fallback fires and the negative result is a clean,
publishable falsification.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{A coupled selection-and-routing method from one attribution.} Selection
$=$ sum, routing $=$ support of the \emph{same} endpoint+NAIT-residualized attribution,
tied by an exact identity (Sec.~\ref{sec:method}, Lemma~\ref{lem:couple}). NAIT cannot
express this: it discards the per-layer axis it computes.
\item \textbf{A well-posed, fully-specified routing estimand.} The routing object is a
\emph{pool-conditional policy value} $\Vpol(\pi)$ over whole runs (no per-example
potential outcome, no SUTVA), with the policy domain pinned to $\Aset$, the mask$\to$rank
map a closed-form, deterministic, \emph{score-free} largest-remainder apportionment, and
every control a single deterministic map of $x$.
\item \textbf{An interventional routing certificate.} Six real masked-LoRA arms decided
by an intersection-union test over a paired studentized bootstrap-$t$ (no multiplicity
penalty; valid at the least-favorable configuration). The Intersection-Union construction
(Berger 1982) is exact \emph{conditional on} valid level-$\alpha$ component tests; the
implemented per-contrast inference is asymptotic (paired bootstrap-$t$), so the
finite-sample level is approximate.
\item \textbf{A faithful NAIT comparator and an honest budget.} NAIT reproduced over the
full released $\Lset$ (base Eq.~5), both base variants run, the stronger gating the
headline; a measured compute ledger with an extraction-parity kill.
\item \textbf{Theory.} Strict propriety of the Brier factuality calibrator; dual-ridge
feasibility/equivalence; residual orthogonality; the coupling identity; the
intersection-union size bound; and the closed-testing FWER bound (Sec.~\ref{sec:theory}).
\item \textbf{A locked, falsifiable protocol with a green implementation.} Datasets,
models, baselines, multiplicity, margins, and kill-gates are pinned; the modules are
implemented as do-not-run pure-Python with a green 93-test harness
(\texttt{server.authorized=false}; Sec.~\ref{sec:setup}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
% =====================================================================

\paragraph{Position relative to the base paper (NAIT).}
NAIT~\cite{nait2026} selects instruction examples by neuron-level activation alignment,
collapsing each layer to a principal direction and \emph{summing} the per-layer
projections over the released decoder set. We do not claim neuron-aware selection is new:
NAIT is both our must-beat comparator and our control. The endpoint signature $\phend$
and the faithful NAIT layer-sum $s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}$ (over $\Lset$) both enter our full
control block, so the contribution lives entirely in the part of the attribution that
survives partialling out NAIT's own readout; by construction it cannot be an artifact of
re-encoding the endpoint or NAIT score. Crucially, NAIT \emph{scores} over the full
$\Lset$ (base Eq.~5), so our faithful comparator does too; LATTICE-R \emph{routes} only
over the anchor set $\Aset$. These are different objects (comparator score vs.\ capacity
mask) and there is no conflict.

\paragraph{Instruction-tuning data selection (and its threat model).}
Quality- and diversity-based selection~\cite{cao2023instruction,zhou2023lima},
importance resampling~\cite{xie2023dsir}, and influence/gradient
attribution~\cite{koh2017influence,pruthi2020tracin,xia2024less} select examples without
reading internal reasoning trajectories. LESS~\cite{xia2024less} and
TracIn~\cite{pruthi2020tracin} attribute utility through gradients; we reuse an
influence-on-validation attribution only to \emph{define} our observational outcome
$Y_{\mathrm{obs}}$ (so $Y_{\mathrm{obs}}$ is method-neutral), then ask whether a
trajectory representation predicts that utility beyond endpoints, and whether \emph{routing}
on it helps. We adopt the threat model of \cite{diddee2024chasing}: selection strategies
generalize poorly and often fail to beat random, with selection cost sometimes exceeding
full fine-tuning. Random, full-data, zero-shot, and gradient-representation selectors are
therefore all required baselines, and Gate R2 is gated on beating the \emph{strongest}
comparator, not NAIT alone. Coreset/diversity methods~\cite{sener2018coreset} are
neighbours; we cite the classic core-set formulation rather than an unverified recent
identifier.

\paragraph{Layer-/neuron-selective and rank-adaptive tuning (the routing prior art).}
AdaLoRA~\cite{zhang2023adalora} reallocates LoRA rank \emph{globally during training} by
an SVD-importance score; Surgical Fine-Tuning~\cite{lee2023surgical} tunes one
\emph{fixed} layer block for a whole task; NeFT~\cite{xu2024neft} tunes selected neurons,
task-globally. LATTICE-R differs on two axes simultaneously: its allocation is
\emph{per example} (conditioned on that example's internal evidence) and \emph{a priori}
(decided before training from the attribution), and it is \emph{coupled} to the selection
score. AdaLoRA, a global-surgical block, and (optionally) NeFT are required R1 routing
arms, projected to the same integer capacity grid as every other arm, so that ``routing
on $\psivec$ beats global/adaptive rank allocation'' is measured, not assumed.

\paragraph{Inference, distances, calibration, and submodular selection.}
We rely on partialling-out~\cite{frisch1933partial,lovell1963seasonal}, its modern
double-ML form~\cite{chernozhukov2018double}, and ridge
regularization~\cite{hoerl1970ridge} for the residual estimand;
permutation/bootstrap inference~\cite{good2005permutation,efron1993bootstrap,hall1988bootstrap}
and BCa intervals~\cite{efron1987bca} for valid out-of-sample testing; the
intersection-union test~\cite{berger1982iut} for every ``beats all'' decision and a
graphical sequentially-rejective scheme~\cite{bretz2009graphical,holm1979simple} for
cross-family gating; strictly proper scoring~\cite{brier1950verification,gneiting2007strictly}
and calibration error~\cite{guo2017calibration} for the factuality gate; intraclass
correlation~\cite{shrout1979intraclass} for outcome reliability; Fisher-based
stability~\cite{kirkpatrick2017ewc} for retention; the Nemhauser--Wolsey--Fisher
$(1-1/e)$ guarantee~\cite{nemhauser1978submodular} with lazy
greedy~\cite{minoux1978accelerated,wei2015submodular} for selection; sliced-Wasserstein
distances~\cite{bonneel2015sliced,kolouri2019gsw,villani2009optimal} and kernel
MMD~\cite{gretton2012mmd} for the distributional term; and LoRA~\cite{hu2022lora} for
matched-budget tuning. Transformer-circuit analyses~\cite{elhage2021mathematical}
motivate treating the residual stream as a path. Our novelty is not any single tool but
their composition into a coupled selection-and-routing method certified by a policy-value
routing test.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
% =====================================================================

Dependency order: faithful NAIT (control $+$ comparator) $\to$ trajectory operator
$\Tmat(x)$ $\to$ endpoint+NAIT-residualized attribution $\psivec$ and the \emph{coupling
identity} $\to$ routing \emph{policy} $\pi_\psi$ over $\Aset$ with a fully-specified
mask$\to$rank map and its \emph{pool-conditional} policy value $\Vpol(\pi)$ $\to$
compute-matched method comparison. Two outcome symbols are kept distinct and \emph{never}
conflated: $Y_{\mathrm{obs}}(x)$ is the observational per-example utility (the R0
regression target that $\psivec$ is fit to predict), and $\Utr(\pi)$ is the held-out
\emph{training-outcome} functional (what R1/R2 score). The implemented modules realizing
each block are named inline and listed in Table~\ref{tab:modules}.

\subsection{Notation and the faithful NAIT baseline}
For a transformer with released decoder set $\Lset$ (the NAIT base model is LLaMA-2-7b,
so $|\Lset|=32$) and hidden width $d$, write the residual-stream activation at token
position $p$, layer $\ell$ as $h_\ell(p)\in\R^d$. Over the anchor set $\Aset\subseteq
\Lset$ ($|\Aset|=8$, fixed on validation), the endpoint signature is
\begin{equation}
\phend(x)=\operatorname*{concat}_{\ell\in\Aset}\big[\,h_\ell(p_{\mathrm{start}}),\,h_\ell(p_{\mathrm{end}})\,\big]\in\R^{2d|\Aset|}.
\label{eq:phiend}
\end{equation}
\textbf{Faithful NAIT over the full $\Lset$} (\texttt{baselines/nait\_layerwise.py};
base Eqs.~2--6). For every $\ell\in\Lset$ form $\Delta A^{(\ell)}(P_i)=A^{(\ell)}(t_K)-
A^{(\ell)}(t_1)$ (Eq.~2), $v_\ell=\mathrm{PCA}_1(\{\Delta A^{(\ell)}(P_i)\}_i)$ (Eq.~3),
sign-aligned by $v_\ell\leftarrow-v_\ell$ if $\mu_{\mathrm{diff}}\cdot v_\ell<0$ (Eq.~4),
and score
\begin{equation}
s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}(y)=\sum_{\ell=1}^{L}\big(A^{(\ell)}(y)\cdot v_\ell\big),\qquad
\mathrm{select}=\mathrm{top\text{-}}k(s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}).
\label{eq:nait}
\end{equation}
Both base variants (first/last difference, Eq.~2; and the prose token-mean summary) are
reproduced because the base paper is internally inconsistent; the \emph{stronger} is the
comparator. A restricted 8-anchor score $s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}^{\Aset}=\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}
(A^{(\ell)}\cdot v_\ell)$ is a \emph{secondary, gated} diagnostic only, reported after the
full-$\Lset$ reproduction passes its unit check; it is \emph{never} the comparator the
headline uses. The endpoint reconstruction (\texttt{baselines/nait.py}, $\phend$) is
retained as a \emph{distinct} full endpoint control that must not coincide with the
layerwise sum.

\subsection{Trajectory operator}
Let $H(x)=\{h_{\ell,s,t}\}$ index layers $\ell$, decoding steps $s$, tokens $t$.

\paragraph{Distributional term $D_\ell$ (higher moments).}
Collect the per-layer cloud $P_\ell(x)=\{h_{\ell,s,t}\}$ (subsampled to a persisted cap
of $512$ vectors) and compare it to a held-out target cloud $Q_\ell^T$ by the
sliced-Wasserstein-2 distance, estimated with $K{=}64$ persisted random projections:
\begin{equation}
D_\ell(x)=\sw\!\big(P_\ell(x),Q_\ell^T\big),\qquad
\sw(P,Q)=\E_{u\sim\mathrm{Unif}(S^{d-1})}\!\big[W_2^2(u_\#P,\,u_\#Q)\big].
\label{eq:sw2}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Temporal curvature $\kappa_\ell$ (uses ordering).}
With per-step mean path $g_{\ell,s}=\mathrm{mean}_t\,h_{\ell,s,t}$, velocity
$v_{\ell,s}=g_{\ell,s+1}-g_{\ell,s}$, acceleration $a_{\ell,s}=g_{\ell,s+1}-2g_{\ell,s}+
g_{\ell,s-1}$, the normalized bending energy is
\begin{equation}
\kappa_\ell(x)=\frac{1}{S_x-2}\sum_{s=2}^{S_x-1}\frac{\lVert a_{\ell,s}\rVert}{\lVert v_{\ell,s}\rVert\,\lVert v_{\ell,s-1}\rVert+\varepsilon}.
\label{eq:curv}
\end{equation}
The cross-layer signature stacks the per-layer coordinates over $\Aset$:
\begin{equation}
\Tmat(x)=\big(\{D_\ell(x)\}_{\ell\in\Aset},\,\{\kappa_\ell(x)\}_{\ell\in\Aset}\big)\in\R^{2|\Aset|}.
\label{eq:T}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Endpoint+NAIT-residualized attribution and the frozen out-of-sample map}
\label{sec:residual}
Define the per-example \emph{control feature row} for \emph{any} $x$ (in or out of
sample),
\begin{equation}
\Zmat(x)=\big[\,\phend(x),\,s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}(x),\,V_{\mathrm{proj}}(x),\,\mathbf C(x),\,1\,\big]\in\R^{p},
\label{eq:Z}
\end{equation}
where $V_{\mathrm{proj}}(x)\in\R^{|\Lset|}$ collects the per-layer NAIT projections
$A^{(\ell)}(x)\cdot v_\ell$ (summing to $s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}$) and $\mathbf C$ are
covariates (length, difficulty, family one-hots). Penalizing only the wide
$[\phend,V_{\mathrm{proj}}]$ blocks via $\bm\Omega=\mathrm{blockdiag}(\mathbf I\ \text{on}\
[\phend,V_{\mathrm{proj}}],\ \mathbf0\ \text{on}\ [s_{\mathrm{NAIT}},\mathbf C,1])$, we fit
\emph{once} on the training fold and \emph{freeze} the ridge nuisance coefficient maps
(\texttt{analysis/layer\_attribution.py}::\texttt{frozen\_nuisance\_map}),
\begin{equation}
\Blam=\big(\Zmat^\top\Zmat+\lambda\bm\Omega\big)^{+}\Zmat^\top\Tmat\in\R^{p\times2|\Aset|},
\qquad
\mathbf b_\lambda^{Y}=\big(\Zmat^\top\Zmat+\lambda\bm\Omega\big)^{+}\Zmat^\top Y_{\mathrm{obs}}.
\label{eq:Blam}
\end{equation}
The deployed, \emph{out-of-sample} residuals for any $x$ are closed-form functions of $x$
through its own row $\Zmat(x)$ (\texttt{residualize\_out\_of\_sample}):
\begin{equation}
\Ttil(x)=\Tmat(x)-\Zmat(x)\Blam,\qquad \widetilde Y(x)=Y_{\mathrm{obs}}(x)-\Zmat(x)\mathbf b_\lambda^{Y}.
\label{eq:resid}
\end{equation}
On the stacked training rows this reproduces the in-sample residual maker
$\Ttil=(\mathbf I-\Zmat(\Zmat^\top\Zmat+\lambda\bm\Omega)^{+}\Zmat^\top)\Tmat$ exactly, so
no in-sample math changes; the only addition is that $\Ttil(x)$ is now defined for rows
$\Zmat$ never saw (Theorem~\ref{thm:dual}). The orthogonalized trajectory coefficient
(dual / $n$-space form, feasible at 7B width) on the observational target is
\begin{equation}
\hat{\bm\beta}_T=\big(\Ttil^\top\Ttil\big)^{+}\Ttil^\top\widetilde Y,
\label{eq:beta}
\end{equation}
and the registered residual quantity is the partial-$R^2$,
$\mathrm{partial\text{-}}R^2_T=(\mathrm{RSS}_{\mathrm{red}}-\mathrm{RSS}_{\mathrm{full}})/
\mathrm{RSS}_{\mathrm{red}}$, with the overall increment $\Delta R^2_{\mathrm{overall}}$
on the $\mathrm{TSS}$ denominator mapped to the deployment margin.

\paragraph{Per-layer policy score and the coupling identity.}
The per-layer \emph{routing policy score} is the per-layer contribution of $\hat{\bm\beta}_T$
to the out-of-sample residual (\texttt{per\_layer\_policy\_score}):
\begin{equation}
\psi_\ell(x)=\hat\beta_{D,\ell}\,\widetilde D_\ell(x)+\hat\beta_{\kappa,\ell}\,\widetilde\kappa_\ell(x),
\qquad \ell\in\Aset,
\label{eq:psi}
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde D_\ell,\widetilde\kappa_\ell$ are the per-layer coordinates of $\Ttil(x)$.
\begin{lemma}[Coupling identity]
\label{lem:couple}
Because $\Tmat(x)$ stacks the per-layer coordinates and $\hat{\bm\beta}_T$ is the single
orthogonalized coefficient on $\Ttil$,
\[
\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\psi_\ell(x)=\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\big(\hat\beta_{D,\ell}\widetilde D_\ell(x)+\hat\beta_{\kappa,\ell}\widetilde\kappa_\ell(x)\big)=\hat{\bm\beta}_T\cdot\Ttil(x),
\]
for \emph{out-of-sample} $x$, since both sides use the same frozen $\Blam,\hat{\bm\beta}_T$.
\end{lemma}
\noindent Hence the \textbf{selection score} $\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\psi_\ell(x)$ is exactly the
endpoint+NAIT-residualized trajectory prediction $\hat{\bm\beta}_T\cdot\Ttil(x)$, and the
\textbf{routing support} is $\mathrm{supp}(\psivec(x))\subseteq\Aset$: selection is the
\emph{sum}, routing is the \emph{support}, of \emph{one} attribution. The identity is
unit-tested in- and out-of-sample (\texttt{test\_layer\_routing.py}, item (a)). We
emphasize $\psivec$ is a \emph{predictive} policy score, not a causal layer-write effect;
the certification that writing to $\mathrm{supp}(\psivec)$ is the right place is the
policy-value Gate R1, not this decomposition.

\subsection{The routing policy and the fully-specified mask$\to$rank map}
\label{sec:policy}
A routing \emph{policy} maps each selected $x$ to an anchor mask and integer ranks
(\texttt{layer\_function.py}):
\begin{equation}
\pi:x\mapsto\Big(m_{\Aset}(x)\in\{0,1\}^{\Aset},\,\{r_\ell(x)\}_{\ell\in\Aset}\Big),\quad
\textstyle\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}r_\ell(x)=\Rtot,\quad r_\ell(x)=r_0\ \forall\ell\in\Lset\setminus\Aset,
\label{eq:pol}
\end{equation}
so the $|\Lset\setminus\Aset|$ non-anchor layers form a fixed common substrate at rank
$r_0$ that \emph{cancels in every contrast}. The mask$\to$rank map $\texttt{capacity\_match}$
is a \emph{pure function of $(m_{\Aset},\Rtot,\rmax)$ and nothing else}---it reads no
$\psivec$---defined on the feasible domain $\mathrm{DOM}=\{m_{\Aset}:|\mathrm{supp}(m_{\Aset})|\cdot
\rmax\ge\Rtot\}$. Given a feasible mask with support $S$ of size $k$, it is the
largest-remainder (Hamilton) apportionment: even base $q=\lfloor\Rtot/k\rfloor$ on every ON
layer, a $+1$ top-up of the $\mathrm{rem}=\Rtot-kq$ leftover units to the lowest-index ON
layers (deterministic ascending-$\ell$ tie-break), and a per-layer cap $\rmax$ enforced
among the same ON layers (no support growth). Feasibility is guaranteed \emph{upstream} by
the single constructor $\texttt{make\_feasible\_mask}(\text{raw},\text{score},\Rtot,\rmax)$,
the \emph{only} place an arm-specific score is read: it lifts the empty mask to
uniform-over-$\Aset$ and completes any under-$k_{\min}$ mask ($k_{\min}=\lceil\Rtot/\rmax\rceil$)
with the highest-score OFF anchors. Thus $\texttt{capacity\_match}$ has \emph{no infeasible
branch} and is total on $\mathrm{DOM}$; with $\rmax$ pinned so $|\Aset|\cdot\rmax\ge\Rtot$
(Sec.~\ref{sec:setup}), $U_{\mathrm{train}}(\pi)$ is one implementation-independent
functional. $\pi_\psi$ instantiates the raw mask $1[\psi_\ell(x)\ge\tau_{\mathrm{sel}}]$,
lifts it via $\texttt{make\_feasible\_mask}$ (score $=\psivec$), then applies
$\texttt{capacity\_match}$.
\begin{lemma}[Feasibility / no-overflow; $\texttt{capacity\_match}$ is total and conserving on $\mathrm{DOM}$]
\label{lem:feas}
For every $m_{\Aset}\in\mathrm{DOM}$, $q=\lfloor\Rtot/k\rfloor\le\Rtot/k\le\rmax$; after the
even-base and largest-remainder steps every ON layer carries $q$ or $q+1$. If $q<\rmax$
then $q+1\le\rmax$ and the cap step is a no-op; if $q=\rmax$ then $\Rtot=k\rmax$ and
$\mathrm{rem}=0$, so again no layer exceeds the cap. In both cases $\sum_{\ell}r_\ell=\Rtot$,
no support is grown, and the output is a deterministic function of $(m_{\Aset},\Rtot,\rmax)$.
\end{lemma}

\subsection{The pool-conditional policy value and the kill-gates}
\label{sec:gates}
The held-out training-outcome functional is the retention-adjusted target utility of the
model obtained by training the selected set under per-example masks$+$ranks $\{\pi(x)\}$
(with the fixed $r_0$ substrate on $\Lset\setminus\Aset$), evaluated on the frozen
held-out split. The \emph{policy value} is the \emph{pool-conditional} seed-expectation,
and the confirmatory estimand is the per-control gap:
\begin{equation}
\Vpol(\pi)=\E_{\mathrm{seed}}\big[\Utr(\pi)\mid P_{\mathrm{train}},P_{\mathrm{val}},P_{\mathrm{dep}}\big],
\qquad g_c=\Vpol(\pi_\psi)-\Vpol(\pi_c).
\label{eq:V}
\end{equation}
The expectation is over training seeds only; the three pools are held at their single
locked partition (Sec.~\ref{sec:setup}). The treated unit is the whole run, so there is
\textbf{no per-example potential outcome and no cross-example SUTVA}. The seed-mean
$\widehat\Vpol(\pi)=S^{-1}\sum_s U_{\mathrm{train},s}(\pi)$ is unbiased for $\Vpol(\pi)$, and the
bootstrap-$t$ below resamples exactly the seed randomness it averages, so estimand and
estimator are aligned and the claim is scoped to the locked pools.

\paragraph{Gate R0 (mechanism certificate; \texttt{analysis/residual\_test.py}).}
Reject ``$\Tmat$ adds nothing given $[\phend,\mathrm{NAIT}(\Lset)]$'' iff the
conditional-null block permutation on the orthogonalized residuals $\Ttil$ (within
family$\times$fold strata; $P{=}5000$; frozen cross-fit nuisance, $K{=}10$; no ridge refit
inside a permutation) gives $p<\alpha_{R0}$ \emph{and} the cluster BCa-$95\%$ CI of
$\mathrm{partial\text{-}}R^2_T$ excludes the floor, stable across $r\in\{8,16,32\}$ PCA
poles. \emph{Fail $\Rightarrow$ the method reduces to NAIT; report a clean null}
($\texttt{stop\_main\_novelty\_claim}$).

\paragraph{Gate R1 (interventional routing certificate; \texttt{analysis/routing\_intervention.py}).}
On $P_{\mathrm{val}}$, six real masked-LoRA arms over $\Aset$ (shared $r_0$, shared
$\texttt{capacity\_match}$, shared seeds, compute-matched): $\pi_\psi$; $\pi_{\mathrm{unif}}$
(all anchors ON); $\pi_{\mathrm{shuf}}$ (a fixed within-stratum permutation of the anchor
labels applied to $\psivec$); $\pi_{\mathrm{rand}}$ (a deterministic pseudo-random subset of
$\Aset$ matched in cardinality to $\pi_\psi$); $\pi_{\mathrm{global}}$ (a fixed global
block $A_{\mathrm{glob}}$, surgical-style); $\pi_{\mathrm{ada}}$ (AdaLoRA importance from a
fixed warm-up). \textbf{Every control is a single deterministic map of $x$}: all
randomness is driven by fixed, pre-registered seeds combined with the example's stable
content hash $H(x)$, so the randomization is \emph{part of the policy definition, not part
of the $\Vpol(\pi)$ training-seed expectation}; $\pi_{\mathrm{ada}}$'s importance is frozen
by a \emph{separate} seed $\mathrm{seed}_{\mathrm{ada}}$ run once \emph{outside} that
expectation. The composite ``$\pi_\psi$ beats every control by $\delta_{R1}$'' is an
intersection-union alternative,
\begin{equation}
H_0^{R1}=\bigcup_c\{g_c\le\delta_{R1}\},\qquad
H_1^{R1}=\bigcap_c\{g_c>\delta_{R1}\},
\label{eq:iutnull}
\end{equation}
decided by the intersection-union test~\cite{berger1982iut}: reject iff \emph{each}
per-control one-sided lower bound $L_c(\alpha)$ exceeds $\delta_{R1}$, every $L_c$ from a
paired studentized bootstrap-$t$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:inference}), at the \emph{marginal} level
$\alpha$---\emph{no} penalty across the five controls, \emph{no} shared min-over-controls
quantile. The reported simultaneous lower bound is $L_{R1}=\min_c L_c(\alpha)$. \emph{Fail
$\Rightarrow$ drop routing, keep selection-only} ($\texttt{drop\_routing\_keep\_selection}$).

\paragraph{Gate R2 (compute-matched method win; \texttt{analysis/matched\_budget.py}).}
At a \emph{measured} compute-matched budget (\texttt{cost\_model.py} ledger), LATTICE-R's
$\Utr$ must beat the stronger full-$\Lset$ NAIT variant \emph{and} every baseline by the
locked margins. The R2-target ``beats every comparator'' claim is, like R1, an
intersection-union test over the baseline set ($\delta_{\mathrm{target}}$). The remaining
sub-claims are single margin tests: a \emph{lower} bound above $\delta_{\mathrm{rel}}$ for
the relative-win margin, and an \emph{upper} bound below the ceiling for the
non-inferiority drift/cost margins $\delta_{\mathrm{ret}},\delta_{\mathrm{hall}},
\delta_{\mathrm{cost}}$; \emph{every} sub-claim must pass. \emph{Fail $\Rightarrow$ no
method-win claim} ($\texttt{no\_method\_win\_claim}$).

\paragraph{Gate R3 (honest two-sided cost).} \texttt{cost\_model.py} reports the
extraction-parity multiplier ($>2.0\times\Rightarrow$ high-cost-analysis) and the
training-side ledger; a ``training savings'' claim is licensed \emph{only} if a measured
FLOP/wall-clock reduction with masked adapters actually skipped (skip-flag true) is
recorded.

\paragraph{Descriptive per-layer notion (not causal).} If a per-layer summary is desired,
it is a run-level leave-one-anchor-layer policy-value difference $\Delta_\ell^{\mathrm{LOL}}
=\Vpol(\pi_{\mathrm{full}})-\Vpol(\pi_{-\ell})$ with the explicit $\texttt{capacity\_match}$
redistribution, labeled \emph{descriptive, not causal evidence}, and \emph{outside} the
confirmatory $\alpha$. The off-manifold layer-ablation profile $J_{c,\ell}$ may enter the
router prior only if it passes a validation gate against $\Delta_\ell^{\mathrm{LOL}}$; else
it is dropped and the router uses $\psivec$ alone.

\subsection{Valid inference for the mechanism certificate and the ``beats all'' maxima}
\label{sec:inference}
\textbf{R0 (cross-fit conditional-null permutation).} For each of $K{=}10$
family-stratified folds, fit $\Blam,\mathbf b_\lambda^{Y},\lambda$ on the other nine and
form the out-of-fold residuals; permute the rows of $\Ttil$ relative to $\widetilde Y$
within family$\times$fold strata, recompute the held-out $\mathrm{partial\text{-}}R^2_T$,
and report the add-one $p$-value; bracket by cluster BCa ($B{=}2000$). No
$F$-distribution $p$-value is ever used inferentially.

\noindent\textbf{Per-contrast inference (R1/R2): paired studentized bootstrap-$t$.} For
control/comparator $c$, with per-seed paired differences $d_{c,s}=U_{\mathrm{train},s}(\pi_\psi)-
U_{\mathrm{train},s}(\pi_c)$, gap $\hat g_c=\mathrm{mean}_s\,d_{c,s}$, seed standard error
$\mathrm{se}$, resample seeds with replacement, recompute $t_c^{*(b)}=(\hat g_c^{*(b)}-\hat
g_c)/\mathrm{se}^{*(b)}$, and form the one-sided lower bound
\begin{equation}
L_c(\alpha)=\hat g_c-t^{*}_{c,1-\alpha}\,\mathrm{se}.
\label{eq:Lc}
\end{equation}
This is \emph{asymptotically valid}~\cite{hall1988bootstrap}; we claim \emph{only}
asymptotic validity (an earlier exact-finite-$S$ sign-flip claim is withdrawn---i.i.d.\
seeds make the $d_{c,s}$ i.i.d.\ but not symmetric about their mean). The ``beats all''
decision is the IUT of Eq.~\eqref{eq:iutnull}: reject iff $L_c(\alpha)>\delta$ for every
$c$, with $L_{R1}=\min_c L_c(\alpha)$ (and identically for R2-target).

\section{Theory}
\label{sec:theory}
% =====================================================================
These results are discharged at the design stage (they are mathematical properties of the
estimators, independent of data); the corresponding numeric sanity checks (the Brier
identity, the coupling identity, capacity conservation, the IUT size at the
least-favorable configuration, submodularity) are \emph{formula evaluations in the green
unit harness, not evidence}.

\subsection{Strict propriety of the Brier factuality calibrator}
\begin{theorem}[Strict propriety]
\label{thm:brier}
Score the calibrator output $q\in[0,1]$ against a label $y\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(p)$ by
the Brier rule $\ell_{\mathrm{Brier}}(q,y)=(q-y)^2$. The population risk
$\E[(q-y)^2]=(q-p)^2+p(1-p)$ is uniquely minimized at $q=p$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
$p(q-1)^2+(1-p)q^2=q^2-2pq+p=(q-p)^2+p(1-p)$; the term $p(1-p)$ is independent of $q$ and
$(q-p)^2\ge0$ with equality iff $q=p$; taking expectation over contexts preserves
strictness.
\end{proof}
\noindent Propriety proves \emph{calibration}, not eval-drift prediction; the factuality
gate G6 therefore separately requires rank/linear correlation with held-out drift and an
ECE ceiling before $\lambda_f$ may be non-zero (else $\lambda_f:=0$). None of this enters
the confirmatory routing $\alpha$.

\subsection{Dual feasibility of the frozen residual maker}
\begin{theorem}[Dual equivalence and out-of-sample feasibility]
\label{thm:dual}
Fix a train fold with penalized endpoint/NAIT design $\bm\Phi$, unpenalized design
$\mathbf U=[s_{\mathrm{NAIT}},\mathbf C,1]$, target $y$, penalty $\lambda>0$. The ridge fit
$\min_{a,b}\lVert y-\bm\Phi a-\mathbf U b\rVert^2+\lambda\lVert a\rVert^2$ has the same
fitted values---hence the same coefficient map $\Blam$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:Blam}---as the dual
system with $a=\bm\Phi^\top\alpha$, $\mathbf K=\bm\Phi\bm\Phi^\top+\lambda\mathbf I$:
$(\mathbf U^\top\mathbf K^{-1}\mathbf U)b=\mathbf U^\top\mathbf K^{-1}y$,
$\alpha=\mathbf K^{-1}(y-\mathbf U b)$. The dual touches the endpoint/NAIT width only
through the $N\times N$ Gram; its cost is $O(N^2p+N^3)$, independent of $2d|\Aset|+|\Lset|$,
so the full control is feasible at 7B width and $\Blam$ applies to any out-of-sample row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Stationarity in $a$ gives $a\in\mathrm{row}(\bm\Phi)$, $a=\bm\Phi^\top\alpha$ and
$(\bm\Phi\bm\Phi^\top+\lambda\mathbf I)\alpha=y-\mathbf U b$; stationarity in $b$ is the
unpenalized normal equation; eliminating $\alpha$ yields the Schur-reduced $b$-equation.
Both solve the same strictly convex program ($\lambda>0$), so the fitted values coincide;
the Gram is the only object scaling with the control width, and $\Blam$ is a fixed
$p\times2|\Aset|$ matrix evaluable on any $\Zmat(x)$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Residual orthogonality}
\begin{lemma}[The residual estimand is orthogonal to the endpoint/NAIT geometry]
\label{lem:resid}
With $\Ttil=\Tmat-\Zmat\Blam$ and $\widetilde Y=Y_{\mathrm{obs}}-\Zmat\mathbf b_\lambda^{Y}$,
as $\lambda\to\infty$ on the penalized block $\hat{\bm\beta}_T$ measures only the
association between $Y_{\mathrm{obs}}$ and $\Tmat$ orthogonal to
$[\phend,s_{\mathrm{NAIT}},V_{\mathrm{proj}},\mathbf C,1]$, and
$\mathrm{partial\text{-}}R^2_T=0$ whenever $\Tmat$ is a linear function of that control
block.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
By Frisch--Waugh--Lovell~\cite{frisch1933partial,lovell1963seasonal}, the joint coefficient
of $\Tmat$ equals that from regressing $\widetilde Y$ on $\Ttil$; ridge on the penalized
block is FWL with a shrinkage operator~\cite{hoerl1970ridge}, and the unpenalized block is
projected out exactly. If $\Tmat=[\,\cdot\,]M$, then $\Ttil\to\mathbf0$ on the spanned
directions and the partial-$R^2$ vanishes.
\end{proof}
\noindent This is the formal content of ``beyond NAIT'': because $s_{\mathrm{NAIT}}$ and the
per-layer $V_{\mathrm{proj}}$ are \emph{in} the control, a residual NAIT's own layer-sum
explains cannot pass R0.

\subsection{First-moment pooling is provably insufficient}
\begin{proposition}[Curvature is not a function of the first-moment pool]
\label{prop:curv}
There exist step-sequences with identical per-layer first-moment summaries but different
$\kappa_\ell$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
A reordering of the steps leaves the activation-multiset mean invariant but changes the
ordered second differences $a_{\ell,s}$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:curv} whenever the path is
non-collinear, so $\kappa_\ell$ is not a function of the first-moment pool. NAIT's
first/last difference and token-mean summaries are exactly such first-moment summaries.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The intersection-union test is exact conditional on valid component tests}
\begin{proposition}[IUT size; Berger 1982]
\label{prop:iut}
Let $H_0=\bigcup_{c=1}^{J}\{g_c\le\delta\}$ and let each component reject iff
$L_c(\alpha)>\delta$ with $L_c(\alpha)$ a level-$\alpha$ lower confidence bound for $g_c$.
The IUT rejects $H_0$ iff all $J$ components reject. Then for any $\theta\in H_0$ there is
$c^\star$ with $g_{c^\star}\le\delta$, and
$P_\theta(\text{reject})\le P_\theta(L_{c^\star}(\alpha)>\delta)\le P_\theta(L_{c^\star}(\alpha)
>g_{c^\star})\le\alpha$. Hence the IUT has size $\le\alpha$ with \emph{no} multiplicity
correction and for every $J$, regardless of dependence among contrasts. The supremum is at
the least-favorable configuration ($g_{c^\star}=\delta$, others $\to\infty$)---exactly where
a min-over-controls shared quantile is anti-conservative.
\end{proposition}
\noindent The Intersection-Union construction is therefore \emph{exact conditional on}
each $L_c(\alpha)$ being a valid level-$\alpha$ component bound. Our implemented
$L_c(\alpha)$ is the per-contrast bootstrap-$t$ bound~\cite{hall1988bootstrap}, which is
only \emph{asymptotically} valid (i.i.d.\ seeds make the $d_{c,s}$ i.i.d.\ but not
symmetric, so no finite-$S$ exactness is available); the IUT thus inherits asymptotic, not
finite-sample, validity, and the finite-sample level is approximate. What the union bound
\emph{does} guarantee unconditionally is that no multiplicity penalty is incurred for any
$J$ under arbitrary dependence among contrasts. This is unit-checked: the per-contrast test
attains nominal level on a null DGP, and the IUT holds size $\le\alpha$ at the
single-binding-control configuration when fed valid component bounds
(\texttt{test\_layer\_routing.py}, item (c)).

\subsection{Closed-testing FWER on the corrected leaves}
\begin{theorem}[FWER bound]
\label{thm:fwer}
On the confirmatory family $\{H_0^{R0},H_0^{R1},H_0^{G2t},H_0^{G2r},H_0^{G2h},H_0^{G2c}\}$,
with the pinned graphical scheme~\cite{bretz2009graphical} $R0\to R1\to G2t\to
\{G2r,G2h,G2c\}$ (weights $w_0{=}1$, edges $1.0$, split $(0.34,0.33,0.33)$, recycle $1.0$),
with $R0$ tested by the frozen-nuisance within-stratum block-permutation
partial-$R^2$ test (\S\ref{sec:inference}), the two union-null leaves $R1,G2t$ each tested
\emph{in every intersection that contains them} by the full-union IUT (Prop.~\ref{prop:iut}),
and each drift/cost singleton by the asymptotic bootstrap-$t$ at its weighted-Holm $\alpha$,
the closed test controls the family-wise error rate at $\alpha=0.05$ (asymptotically).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Each leaf local test holds its level: R0 by the frozen-nuisance within-stratum
exchangeability of the permutation; every union-null leaf by the full-union IUT, whose size
is $\le$ its node $\alpha$ for the full union null regardless of the other members of the
intersection (Prop.~\ref{prop:iut}); each single margin leaf by the bootstrap-$t$. A
\emph{sub-scoped} IUT (a proper subset of a union's components) is forbidden because it
tests a strictly smaller hypothesis and is anti-conservative when the binding component is
omitted. By the closed-testing principle, an elementary $H_i$ is rejected only if all
intersections containing $i$ reject at their level; requiring the full union inside every
such intersection only shrinks the rejection region, so $P(\text{reject any true }H_0)\le
\alpha$. The data-dependent ``max over arms'' cannot leak type-I error because the IUT
requires \emph{every} arm to clear the margin.
\end{proof}
\noindent The shortcut/brute-force equivalence over all $2^6-1=63$ intersections, including
the IUT leaves and the least-favorable single-binding-control configuration, is asserted in
code (\texttt{analysis/closed\_testing.py}; \texttt{test\_layer\_routing.py}, item (f)).

\subsection{Selection objective: monotone-submodular $(1-1/e)$ guarantee}
\begin{theorem}[Greedy approximation]
\label{thm:submod}
With per-example utility $u(x)=\max(0,\sum_{\ell\in\Aset}\psi_\ell(x)-\lambda_r\hat r(x)-
\lambda_f\hat f(x))$ and the facility-location coverage $C(S)=\sum_{e}w_e\max_{x\in S}
\sigma(x,e)$, the objective $F(S)=\sum_{x\in S}u(x)+\mu C(S)$ ($u\ge0,w_e\ge0,\sigma\in[0,1],
\mu\ge0$) is monotone submodular; greedy returns $F(S_{\mathrm{greedy}})\ge(1-1/e)F(S^\star)$,
and lazy greedy returns the identical set.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
$\sum_{x\in S}u(x)$ is modular and monotone; each $\max_{x\in S}\sigma(x,e)$ is monotone
with non-increasing marginals (submodular); a non-negative weighted sum preserves
monotone-submodularity, so the Nemhauser--Wolsey--Fisher
bound~\cite{nemhauser1978submodular} applies and lazy greedy~\cite{minoux1978accelerated}
gives the same set. Routing is applied \emph{after} selection, so it never enters $F$ and
the guarantee is untouched.
\end{proof}

\emph{Factuality (G6):} $\lambda_f$ may be non-zero only if, on a held-out slice, Spearman
$\rho(\hat f,\text{drift})\ge0.3$ and Pearson $r\ge0.3$ (one-sided lower $95\%$ CI $>0$) and
ECE $\le0.1$~\cite{guo2017calibration}; else $\lambda_f:=0$. \emph{Outcome reliability
(G7):} before the R0 regression on the influence proxy $Y_{\mathrm{obs}}$, require
$\mathrm{ICC}(2,1)\ge0.6$ across $\ge3$ seeds~\cite{shrout1979intraclass} and Spearman
$\rho(Y_{\mathrm{obs}},\Delta_{\mathrm{retrain}})\ge0.3$ (lower $95\%$ CI $>0$); else fall
back to the direct retrain-delta and do not run the proxy regression. \emph{$J$-validation:}
$J_{c,\ell}$ enters the router prior only if it correlates with $\Delta_\ell^{\mathrm{LOL}}$;
else dropped.

\paragraph{Measured compute ledger (\texttt{cost\_model.py}).}
Per arm: parameter count, optimizer-state slots, \emph{realized} FLOPs (measured, not
rank-inferred), wall-clock, batch/accumulation policy, and a skip-flag (whether masked
adapters are excluded from the backward pass). R2's matched budget requires equality within
a locked tolerance on params, optimizer slots, and realized FLOPs; the shared $r_0$
substrate and shared $\texttt{capacity\_match}$ reduce the match to $\Aset$. Equal rank is
\emph{not} equal compute; an extraction-parity multiplier $>2.0\times$ declares
high-cost-analysis with no deployment claim.

\paragraph{Locked symbols, thresholds, and seeds (pre-registered design choices).}
All pre-registered \emph{decision} thresholds are \textbf{locked now} in
\texttt{configs/experiments/lattice\_v5.yaml}; they are design choices fixed before any
run, \emph{not} results, so the analysis cannot be tuned post hoc. Only genuinely
empirical quantities (resolved checkpoint SHAs, measured FLOPs, observed gaps) remain
\texttt{DATA\_NEEDED}. Shared seeds $0..19$ (\texttt{configs/seeds/paper\_20.txt});
10-fold family-stratified cross-fit for R0; $S\ge20$ seeds for R1/R2;
$B_{\mathrm{bt}}{=}10000$ bootstrap-$t$ resamples. \emph{Capacity (feasibility-coupled):}
$\Rtot{=}64$ allocated over $\Aset$ ($|\Aset|{=}8$, so an even split is the standard
rank-$8$ per anchor), $\rmax{=}16$, $r_0{=}4$ on $\Lset\setminus\Aset$. $\rmax$ is pinned
so $|\Aset|\cdot\rmax{=}128\ge64{=}\Rtot$, hence $1_{\Aset}\in\mathrm{DOM}$,
$k_{\min}{=}\lceil\Rtot/\rmax\rceil{=}4\le|\Aset|$, and $\texttt{capacity\_match}$ is total
and score-free (Lemma~\ref{lem:feas}). \emph{Margins (normalized $[0,1]$ utility):}
$\delta_{R1}{=}\delta_{\mathrm{target}}{=}0.01$ (one normalized utility point---a
\emph{meaningful} routing/method win). The margin is a power-justified \emph{design} floor,
not a fitted constant; the back-of-envelope derivation is: with $S{=}20$ paired seeds and a
target per-seed paired-difference SD $\sigma_d\!\approx\!0.02$ on normalized utility, the
standard error of the mean gap is $\mathrm{se}\!=\!\sigma_d/\sqrt{S}\!\approx\!0.02/\sqrt{20}
\!\approx\!0.0045$. At a plausible true gap $g\!\approx\!0.025$ (just over $1\sigma_d$ of
per-seed separation), the non-centrality is
$(g-\delta_{R1})/\mathrm{se}\!=\!(0.025-0.01)/0.0045\!\approx\!3.3$, against a one-sided
$z_{0.95}\!\approx\!1.64$ critical value, giving $\Phi(3.3-1.64)\!=\!\Phi(1.66)\!\approx\!0.95$
asymptotic power, conservatively $\gtrsim\!0.8$ once the finite-$S$ bootstrap-$t$ quantile
inflation is accounted for. A smaller margin would not be practically meaningful; a larger
one would demand implausibly large true gaps to clear at $S{=}20$. $\delta_{\mathrm{rel}}{=}0$
(non-negativity guard atop $\delta_{\mathrm{target}}$), non-inferiority ceilings
$\delta_{\mathrm{ret}}{=}\delta_{\mathrm{hall}}{=}0.02$ and $\delta_{\mathrm{cost}}{=}0.05$
(tolerable drift/cost). \emph{Floors/gates:} partial-$R^2$ floor
$\texttt{floor\_partial}{=}0.01$ (the residual must explain $\ge1\%$ of residual variance
beyond $[\phend,\mathrm{NAIT}(\Lset)]$), $\rho_J{=}0.30$ (moderate monotone agreement),
$\tau_{\mathrm{sel}}$ the $P_{\mathrm{train}}$ quantile yielding mean raw anchor
cardinality $\bar k{=}4$. \emph{Control specifics:} fixed seeds
$\mathrm{seed}_{\mathrm{rand}},\mathrm{seed}_{\mathrm{shuf}},\mathrm{seed}_{\mathrm{ada}}$
(integers outside $0..19$), the surgical block $A_{\mathrm{glob}}$ as the top-$4$ anchors
by pooled $|\psi_\ell|$, AdaLoRA warm-up $W_{\mathrm{ada}}{=}200$ steps. \emph{Pooling:}
$\texttt{pool\_split\_seed}$ fixes the single locked $(P_{\mathrm{train}},P_{\mathrm{val}},
P_{\mathrm{dep}})$ partition; marginal level $\alpha{=}0.05$; ledger tolerance
$2\%$; the $\lambda$ grid is cross-validated per fold. The closed-test $\alpha$-allocation
($w_0{=}1$ on R0, unit edges $R0{\to}R1{\to}G2t$, split $(0.34,0.33,0.33)$, recycle $1.0$)
is the pinned graphical scheme (Sec.~\ref{sec:theory}).

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}
% =====================================================================
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{The residual attribution may be null (R0).} If $[\phend,\mathrm{NAIT}(\Lset)]$
already explains the variance, R0 does not reject and the novelty claim stops. This is the
intended falsifier; the honest prior is that the residual may be small.
\item \textbf{Routing may not beat uniform/AdaLoRA (R1 null).} R1 is a pre-registered
policy-value kill-gate with a clean fallback (drop routing, keep selection-only). Publishable
either way.
\item \textbf{$\psivec$ is predictive, not causal.} $\psivec$ only \emph{proposes} the
policy; the policy value $\Vpol(\pi_\psi)$ (real runs, once executed) would certify it. Ridge/fold instability
that moves $\psivec$ mass without changing prediction fails the $\pi_{\mathrm{shuf}}$ arm.
\item \textbf{No per-example routing effect is claimed.} The estimand is a run-level policy
value; the only per-layer notion is the descriptive $\Delta_\ell^{\mathrm{LOL}}$, labeled
non-causal and outside the confirmatory $\alpha$.
\item \textbf{Scope is the locked pools.} $\Vpol(\pi)$ is the pool-conditional
seed-expectation; the pool-marginal statement requires the separate, non-confirmatory
multi-pool probe with its own multiplicity.
\item \textbf{Per-contrast inference is asymptotic.} We claim only the asymptotic validity of
the paired studentized bootstrap-$t$; the exact-finite-$S$ sign-flip claim is withdrawn, and
the one route to exactness (an actual seed-to-arm randomization) is not adopted.
\item \textbf{Compute matching is fragile.} The ledger requires \emph{measured} FLOP/wall-clock
equality and a skip-flag; unmatched arms are diagnostic-labeled and ``savings'' is forbidden
without a measured reduction. Equal rank is not equal compute.
\item \textbf{Off-manifold $J$.} The layer-ablation profile is validated-or-dropped against a
real anchor layer-freeze policy-value difference.
\item \textbf{Factuality proxy validity is a precondition, not a result.} Brier proves
calibration, not eval-drift prediction; fail G6 $\Rightarrow\lambda_f:=0$.
\item \textbf{The outcome $Y$ is anchored on the endpoint signature.} The LOCI clusters that
define $Y$ are fit on the L2-normalized endpoint signature $\phend$ (Eq.~15)---the same
\emph{full} control that $\psivec$ residualizes against. This is a construct-validity risk: an
outcome partitioned by $\phend$ could share variance with an endpoint-anchored selection in a
way that flatters $\psivec$. We do not claim the $0.3/0.6$ G7 floors (Spearman
$\rho(Y,\Delta_{\mathrm{retrain}})$ and ICC$(2,1)$, with lower-CI $>0$) \emph{guarantee}
substantive proxy validity; they are necessary preconditions, not a certificate, and a reviewer
may legitimately argue the floors are too lax. On G7 failure the influence proxy is dropped for
the direct retrain-delta $Y$ (diagnostic), so the headline never rests on an unvalidated proxy.
\item \textbf{Influence approximation.} First-order LOCI influence can be inaccurate for large
moves; G7 is the explicit precondition---fail $\Rightarrow$ no primary claim on the proxy.
\item \textbf{No empirical claim yet.} Every result is design; the only numeric checks are the
green-harness formula evaluations, which are code-correctness, not evidence.
\item \textbf{The fully-identified claim is narrower than the novel one (the central
exposure).} What R1 identifies and pre-registers is \emph{a particular routing policy beats
its capacity-matched controls given a fixed selection}; the strictly causal, load-bearing
reading of the coupling---``one shared vector, used as both the selection sum and the routing
support, is what carries the gain''---is \emph{not} point-identified by this design and we do
not claim it. We are explicit that this is a deliberate de-scoping, not an oversight: a hostile
reviewer can fairly hold that the identified result is incremental over AdaLoRA/surgical/NAIT,
while the genuinely novel coupling claim is exactly the one we decline to certify. We do not
treat this as resolved by construction. We mitigate, but do not close, the gap on two fronts.
First, $\pi_{\mathrm{shuf}}$ is a genuine (if partial) coupling probe: it breaks the
layer$\leftrightarrow$example correspondence while preserving the $\psivec$ marginal, so if the
per-example structure of $\psivec$ (not merely its marginal) is load-bearing, $\pi_{\mathrm{shuf}}$
should lose---this tests a meaningful fraction of the coupling claim, though it does not isolate
``one vector versus two.'' Second, R0 residualizes against the \emph{wide}
$[\phend,\mathrm{NAIT}(\Lset)]$ control, making the novelty test anti-conservative against
ourselves, so a passing R0 is meaningful and a failing R0 triggers the honest ``reduces to NAIT,
report clean null'' fallback. The residual risk is therefore one of
\emph{significance/identifiability of the interesting claim}, not of soundness: the design is
sound and the falsifiable claim it does make is identified, but whether reviewers credit the
de-scoped contribution as sufficiently novel is venue- and disposition-dependent, and we do not
assert otherwise.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% =====================================================================
We presented LATTICE-R, a Stage-1 Registered Report that turns the per-layer axis NAIT
computes-and-discards into a decision variable: one endpoint+NAIT-residualized trajectory
attribution drives both data selection (its sum) and LoRA layer routing (its support), tied
by an exact coupling identity. We make the routing object a well-posed, fully-specified
\emph{pool-conditional policy value} over whole training runs---no per-example potential
outcome, no SUTVA, a score-free closed-form capacity map, and deterministic
controls---and the design \emph{would certify} the coupling, once the authorized run
executes, with an interventional routing test (six real masked-LoRA arms, an
intersection-union test over a paired bootstrap-$t$). A ridge-FWL mechanism certificate
residualized against NAIT over the full $\Lset$, a measured compute ledger, a locked
three-pool firewall, and a closed-testing graph with an in-document FWER bound complete
the plan. The theory is discharged where provable and the modules are
implemented as do-not-run pure-Python with a green 93-test harness
(\texttt{server.authorized=false}; no run executed). \textbf{Empirical validation follows
the authorized run}; no experimental numbers are claimed here, and a null at any gate is a
clean, publishable falsification.
